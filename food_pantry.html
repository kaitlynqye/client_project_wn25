Maise & Blue Cupboard Food Pantry

Welcome to the Michigan Maize and Blue Cupboard: Food Pantry

The Michigan Maize and Blue Cupboard (MBC) provides a variety of resources to help students meet their basic needs, including access to food, hygiene products, and other support services. The Food Pantry is here to assist students who may be experiencing food insecurity.

Their mission statement: We are committed to providing healthy, nutritious food options to support your academic and personal well-being. No student should have to choose between food and their education.

Food Pantry Services

The Food Pantry provides free groceries, including fresh produce, non-perishable items, and personal care products. The pantry is open to all University of Michigan students, regardless of their financial situation.

  • Groceries: A variety of fresh produce, canned goods, and dry foods are available.
  • Personal Care Products: Hygiene products such as soap, toothpaste, and other essentials.
  • Kitchen and Cooking: Necessary kitchenware such as dishes, silverware, pots & pans, Tupperware, cutting boards, and more.

How to Access the Food Pantry

To access the Food Pantry, you must be a registered University of Michigan student. You can visit the pantry during open hours, and no appointment is necessary. Students are welcome to visit the pantry once a week to pick up food and personal care items.

Maize and Blue Cupboard

The Maize and Blue Cupboard is another critical resource for students at the University of Michigan. It provides food and essential products to students who are experiencing food insecurity. The Maize and Blue Cupboard operates as part of the Michigan Basic Needs Center, and it offers a range of services:

  • Food Pantry: Access non-perishable food, fresh produce, and frozen items.
  • Personal Hygiene Products: Receive essential hygiene items like soap, toothpaste, and sanitary products.
  • Nutrition Education: Participate in workshops and receive resources to help students make informed, healthy food choices.
  • Emergency Assistance: If you are facing an emergency or short-term need, the Maize and Blue Cupboard can provide immediate support.

Students can easily access the Maize and Blue Cupboard by visiting their website. Here's how to interact with the Maize and Blue Cupboard online:

  • Make an Appointment: Visit the Maize and Blue Cupboard website to schedule an appointment and access food and other services.
  • Browse Available Items: View the available food and products that you can pick up during your appointment.
  • Request Emergency Support: If you are in urgent need of food or personal care items, you can request emergency assistance through the website.
  • Get Involved: The Maize and Blue Cupboard welcomes volunteers and donations. Check their website for opportunities to contribute.

Additional Resources

The Michigan Basic Needs Center offers a variety of other resources to support students' basic needs:

  • Financial Assistance: Emergency funds and resources for students experiencing financial hardship.
  • Housing Support: Assistance with finding temporary or long-term housing options.
  • Student Wellness Programs: Support for students' overall well-being, including mental health resources.

For more information, visit the Michigan Maize and Blue Cupboard website.
