Study Spaces on Campus

Where to go to for studying on campus?

Here at the University of Michigan, there are countless study spaces you can attend! Here is a list of some of the students' favorite places to go to, along with a link to their website page and an image of what they look like! Additionally, a quick descriptor of what each study place area is like and who typically goes will be provided below!

List of Kaitlyn's Favorite Study Spots!

Shapiro Library

The Shapiro Undergraduate Library, also known as the Ugli, is a prime location to meet for a group project, find a study nook, or pull a cram session — fueled with food and drink from the first-floor café, or better known as the Mujo Café...

Michigan Union

The Michigan Union is one of the most beloved buildings on Central Campus. Impressive in stature and alive with activity, enter the Michigan Union and you’ll find yourself immediately immersed in the Go Blue! culture...

Michigan League

Similar to the Michigan Union, the Michigan League is another beautiful student union located more on the north end of campus. Also home to student cafes and historic architecture...

Harlan Hatcher Library

The Harlan Graduate Library is a perfect spot to study, complete with many floors of study space and quiet areas. However, the south section, in particular, has secluded spaces where you can focus privately...
